A value stock is a good company on sale.

Imagine a well-made, high-quality product that's temporarily unpopular and marked down. A value stock is the same: it's a share in a solid, profitable company that the market has overlooked or unfairly punished, causing its price to trade below its true, intrinsic worth. Value investors hunt for these hidden gems.

THE BIGGEST PITFALL

Beware the Value Trap

‚ö†Ô∏è

Sometimes, a stock is cheap for a reason.

A "value trap" is a stock that looks like a bargain but is actually a failing company. Its price is low because its business is in permanent trouble. The key challenge for investors is to differentiate a temporarily undervalued gem from a permanently broken business. This requires deep research beyond the basic numbers.
